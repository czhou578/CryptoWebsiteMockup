A Brief History of Crypto

1983

              This was when cryptographer David Chaum received
              the first batch of cash that was cryptographically 
              secured. He founds Digicash soon after, allowing users
              to wirelessly send money to each other that made it 
              otherwise undetectable from the government or a bank.

1996

              The American NSA agency publishes a paper called
              How to Make a Mint: the Cryptography of Anonymous 
              Electronic Cash.It is published in the MIT mailing 
              list, and later in the American Law Review

1998

              Nick Szabo describes bit gold, a conceptual
              framework where money would be sent and received
              based on a proof of work function with solutions
              being cryptographically put together.

2009

              Bitcoin, the famed cryptocurrency that was
              developed by unknown Satoshi Nakamoto was
              released to the public domain, using the SHA-256
              hashing function.

August 2014

              The United Kingdom announces that its
              Treasury had commenced a study on the 
              possible effects that cryptocurrencies could
              have on its national economy. It remained a 
              consultative paper on the subject until 2021.

June 2021

              El Salvador becomes the first 
              country to make Bitcoin legal tender,
              after the congress passed a bill with 62-22
              support.
